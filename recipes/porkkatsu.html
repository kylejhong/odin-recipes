Pork Katsu

Description

Pork Katsu is a Japanese comfort food featuring breaded and deep-fried pork cutlets. Crunchy on the outside and juicy on the inside, it’s commonly served with shredded cabbage and a drizzle of tangy tonkatsu sauce. Simple yet satisfying, it’s a perfect introduction to Japanese home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless pork loin chops
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Shredded cabbage, for serving
  • Cooked white rice, for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. Trim excess fat from pork and pound each chop to 1/2-inch thickness.
  2. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Prepare three shallow bowls: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with panko.
  4. Dredge pork in flour, then dip in egg, then coat thoroughly with panko.
  5. Heat about 1/2 inch of oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Fry pork cutlets for 3–4 minutes per side or until golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Drain on paper towels.
  8. Slice into strips and serve with shredded cabbage and tonkatsu sauce.
  9. Optional: serve with white rice for a full meal.
